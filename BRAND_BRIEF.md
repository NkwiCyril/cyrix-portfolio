## 2. What Cyrix Does

Cyrix is a one-person studio operating across three lanes:

1. **Client work** — full-stack web and app development, AI-augmented products, and creative digital solutions for businesses.
2. **Content** — a blog publishing thoughts on technology, software development, and the future of digital experiences.
3. **Education (upcoming)** — a mentorship program teaching software, AI, and modern development.

The brand needs to feel equally credible as a freelance technologist, a content creator, and an educator. The logo will appear on:

- Site header and footer
- Social media profiles (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram)
- Blog post thumbnails
- Email signatures
- Future mentorship branding
- Possibly merch (stickers, t-shirts)

## 3. Visual Personality

Pick adjectives — these are the ones the brand should feel like:

| Be | Don't be |
|---|---|
| Bold | Cute |
| Confident | Quirky |
| Modern | Retro |
| Technical | Corporate-stiff |
| Minimal | Bare |

The aesthetic is **black background + sharp orange accent**, similar to high-end editorial sites and design studios — think Awwwards-tier portfolios, not bootcamp landing pages.

## 4. Color System

The brand runs on a dark-first palette with a single, decisive accent.

### Primary

| Token | Hex | RGB | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Background (primary)** | `#0a0a0a` | 10, 10, 10 | Near-black; main site background. Use as the logo's natural environment. |
| **Background (elevated)** | `#0f0f0f` | 15, 15, 15 | Slightly raised surfaces (cards, panels). |
| **Foreground / Text** | `#ffffff` | 255, 255, 255 | Headlines and body. |
| **Accent (signature)** | `#f76b34` | 247, 107, 52 | Neon orange. This is THE brand color — the logo's spark, hover states, CTAs, highlights. |
| **Accent (dark)** | `#d45a2a` | 212, 90, 42 | Pressed/hover state for the orange. Also used as a subtle decorative variant. |

### Supporting greys (neutrals)

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `gray-50` | `#fafafa` | Light-mode backgrounds within white sections. |
| `gray-200` | `#e5e5e5` | Borders, dividers on light surfaces. |
| `gray-400` | `#a3a3a3` | Muted body text on dark. |
| `gray-700` | `#404040` | Scrollbar, subtle dividers on dark. |
| `gray-900` | `#171717` | Hover-state surfaces on dark. |
| `gray-950` | `#0a0a0a` | Same as primary background. |

### Color rules for the logo

- The **primary mark** must read on `#0a0a0a` (dark site) and on `#ffffff` (light contexts like email signatures or printed media).
- The orange (`#f76b34`) should appear in the logo somewhere — even as a small accent dot, underline, or single counter — to anchor brand recognition. It does not have to dominate.
- Avoid introducing new colors (green, blue, purple). Stay monochrome + accent.
- **Never** place orange `#f76b34` directly on light-grey backgrounds (`#fafafa`–`#e5e5e5`) — contrast suffers. Use it on black or white.

## 5. Typography

The site already ships with two custom fonts. The logo should harmonize with these, not compete.

| Role | Font | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Logo / wordmark in app** | **Puertos Regular** (custom .woff2) | Currently used for the word "CYRIX" in the site header. Has a slightly geometric, condensed-display feel. |
| **Body & headings** | **Helvena** (custom .woff2, weights 200–900) | Modern sans-serif. Wide weight range — display headings sit at 700–900, body at 400. |

If the logo is a wordmark, the designer can either:
- Continue building on Puertos's geometry (matches what's deployed), **or**
- Propose a custom letterform — provided it shares Puertos's structural DNA (geometric, condensed, slightly futuristic).

If the logo is a monogram or symbol-mark, it should pair cleanly with Helvena Bold for the wordmark beneath it.

## 6. Existing Visual Cues to Reference

Several decorative elements already live on the site — the logo should feel like it belongs in this family:

- **A four-pointed star/sparkle** (`✦`) used as a separator in the marquee and the "Mentorship Coming Soon" badge. This is a candidate motif for the logo.
- **Sharp, square corners** — the site uses almost no rounded corners. Buttons, cards, and dividers are crisp. The logo should match this geometric discipline.
- **Uppercase tracking** — section labels and CTAs use uppercase with wide letter-spacing. The wordmark should look comfortable in all-caps.
- **High-contrast blocks** — the site alternates pure-black and pure-white sections. The logo must work confidently in both.

## 7. Logo Brief — What to Design

### Required deliverables

1. **Primary logo** — wordmark or wordmark + symbol. Horizontal lockup.
2. **Compact mark** — a single letter, monogram, or icon (~1:1 ratio) for favicons, app icons, social avatars (must read at 32×32px).
3. **Light-on-dark version** — primary use case (`#ffffff` or `#f76b34` on `#0a0a0a`).
4. **Dark-on-light version** — for white backgrounds, print, and email.
5. **Monochrome version** — single-color for stamping, embroidery, watermarks.

### Concept directions to explore

The designer is free to interpret, but here are starting angles:

- **Letterform play on the "X"** — the brand name ends in a strong consonant. The X can be sliced, mirrored, or extended into a sparkle/cross motif that echoes the site's `✦` accent.
- **Bracket-style mark** — software/dev-coded references like `[ ]`, `< >`, `{ }`, or `/` — but executed editorially, not as cliché "developer" iconography.
- **C + Y monogram** — a tight geometric ligature for the compact mark.
- **Spark/star integration** — using the existing `✦` sparkle as a dot on the i, an accent next to the wordmark, or a standalone symbol-mark.

### Hard "no"s

- No gradients (the palette is flat).
- No 3D, bevels, or shadows.
- No stock developer/code symbols ( `</>`, `{}`, terminal cursors ) used literally.
- No globe/world imagery.
- No initials-in-a-circle clichés.
- No retro / Y2K / chrome aesthetics.
- Nothing that would look dated in 3 years.

## 8. Tone & Voice (for context)

The brand voice on the site is **direct, capable, and slightly editorial** — not playful, not corporate. Sample copy from the homepage:

> "Unlock Digital Success For Your Business Today"
> "Taking your online presence to the next level"
> "Thoughts on technology, software development, and the future of digital experiences"

The logo should feel like it was made by the same person who wrote that copy: focused, intentional, no decoration for decoration's sake.

---

## 9. Reference Comparables

For mood, not imitation:

- **Linear** — disciplined geometry, single accent color, dark-first.
- **Vercel** — confident wordmark, minimal symbol-mark, geometric.
- **Stripe** — clean type, strong color discipline.
- **Awwwards-winning portfolio sites** circa 2024–2026 — editorial layouts, sharp corners, orange/lime accents on near-black.

Avoid: typical "freelance developer" portfolio logos that lean cute, illustrative, or templated.
